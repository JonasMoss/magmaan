\section*{Purpose}

The companion note \texttt{higher\_order\_discrepancy\_misspec.tex} fixes the
\emph{mean} of the fitted discrepancy under fixed model and distributional
misspecification: $2N\{v(\hat x)-v(x_0)\}\dto Z'Q\Gamma Z$ with
$\E[\hat F]=F_0+n^{-1}[\tr(Q\Gamma)+\beta_W]+o(n^{-1})$, the curvature-corrected
generalized df $\tr(Q\Gamma)$. This note does the complementary object: the
\emph{inertia} of the same $Q\Gamma$. The limiting law is a weighted mixture
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mixture}
  2N\{v(\hat x)-v(x_0)\}\;\dto\;\sum_{j=1}^{m}\lambda_j\,\chi^2_{1,j},
  \qquad \lambda_j=\operatorname{eig}_j(Q\Gamma),
\end{equation}
and the questions are: how many $\lambda_j$ are nonzero (how many $\chi^2_1$ terms
are actually present), how many are positive, and exactly when the count
collapses to the classical $df=m-p$. This is the analytic backing for the
\texttt{spectrum\_size} field of magmaan's \texttt{WeightedProfileRMSEAResult}
/ \texttt{WeightedProfileLRTResult}, which deliberately reports the size of the
positive spectrum separately from the nominal model df because, as shown below,
the two need not agree once an observed-bread or estimated-weight profile method
is used. The single result is an inertia identity
(Theorem~\ref{thm:rank}); three corollaries read off when classical df survives
(\ref{cor:classical}), which channel breaks it (\ref{cor:channels}), and what
the broken case does to the magmaan summaries (\ref{cor:magmaan}).

\section{The profile Hessian}\label{sec:setup}

Fix the notation of the companion note. The moment vector is $s\in\mathbb R^m$
(the saturated statistics, $m=p^\ast(p^\ast+1)/2$ for $p^\ast$ observed
variables), the model is $\sigma(\theta)$, $\theta\in\mathbb R^p$ free after
identification, with Jacobian $\dot\sigma=\partial\sigma/\partial\theta$
($m\times p$, full column rank $p$) and curvature tensor $\ddot\sigma$. The fit
minimizes a moment-quadratic discrepancy with an outer (data) metric $V_{\!o}$
and a projection (model) metric $W_{\!p}$; for a fixed-weight estimator the two
coincide, $V_{\!o}=W_{\!p}=V$, while for a data-weighted estimator they differ.
At the pseudo-true $\theta_0$ write the population residual $e=\sigma(\theta_0)-s_\infty$,
the Gauss--Newton part $A=\dot\sigma'W_{\!p}\dot\sigma$, the
residual--curvature coupling $K=(W_{\!p}e)'\ddot\sigma$, and the discrepancy
Hessian $B=A-K$. The profile Hessian is the curvature-corrected residual-weight
matrix
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Q}
  Q \;=\; V_{\!o}\;-\;W_{\!p}\,\dot\sigma\,B^{-1}\dot\sigma'\,W_{\!p}
  \qquad(\text{$=\tilde U$ of the companion note}),
\end{equation}
in single-metric ($V_{\!o}=W_{\!p}=V$, ULS / fixed-weight GLS) or two-metric form
($V_{\!o}=V_0$ the data metric, $W_{\!p}=W_\ast$ the model metric, ML / GLS).

\begin{remark}[Why $Q$, not the naive sandwich]\label{rem:envelope}
$Q$ is exactly $\tfrac12$ the Hessian of the profiled discrepancy
$s\mapsto\min_\theta F(s,\theta)$ in the moment argument, by the envelope theorem
($\partial_s\,[\text{profiled }F]=2W_{\!p}r(\hat\theta)$, then differentiate again
using $\partial_s\hat\theta=B^{-1}\dot\sigma'W_{\!p}$). It is \emph{not} the naive
plug-in sandwich $(I-\dot\sigma B^{-1}\dot\sigma'W_{\!p})'V_{\!o}
(I-\dot\sigma B^{-1}\dot\sigma'W_{\!p})$ obtained by linearizing
$r(\hat\theta)$ and squaring: that drops the $O_p(n^{-1})$ cross term between the
leading and second-order parts of $r(\hat\theta)$ and so disagrees with $Q$ off
$H_0$ (numerically $\lVert\text{sandwich}-Q\rVert=0.95$ in the check below). The
magmaan primitive stores $Q$, the envelope object, which is the correct one.
\end{remark}

\section{The inertia identity}\label{sec:rank}

Let $\In(\cdot)=(\np,\nz,\nm)$ denote the inertia (numbers of positive, zero,
and negative eigenvalues) of a symmetric matrix. Define the \emph{metric-gap
form}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Atil}
  \tilde A \;=\; \dot\sigma'\,W_{\!p}\,V_{\!o}^{-1}W_{\!p}\,\dot\sigma
  \qquad(p\times p),
\end{equation}
which collapses to $A$ in the single-metric case $V_{\!o}=W_{\!p}$.

\begin{lemma}[Spectral split]\label{lem:split}
With $\tilde G=V_{\!o}^{-1/2}W_{\!p}\dot\sigma$ ($m\times p$, rank $p$),
\[
  Q=V_{\!o}^{1/2}\,(I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G')\,V_{\!o}^{1/2},
\]
the column space $\operatorname{col}(\tilde G)$ is invariant under
$I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G'$, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:spectrum}
  \operatorname{spec}(I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G')
  =\underbrace{\{\,1\,\}}_{\times(m-p)}\;\cup\;\{\,1-\nu_j:\,j=1,\dots,p\,\},
  \qquad \nu_j=\operatorname{eig}_j(B^{-1}\tilde A).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On $\operatorname{col}(\tilde G)^{\perp}$ we have $\tilde G'u=0$, so
$(I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G')u=u$: an eigenvalue $1$ with multiplicity $m-p$. For
$v=\tilde G w$, $(I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G')\tilde G w
=\tilde G(I-B^{-1}\tilde G'\tilde G)w=\tilde G(I-B^{-1}\tilde A)w$, since
$\tilde G'\tilde G=\dot\sigma'W_{\!p}V_{\!o}^{-1}W_{\!p}\dot\sigma=\tilde A$. Thus
$\operatorname{col}(\tilde G)$ is invariant and the operator acts there as
$I-B^{-1}\tilde A$, with eigenvalues $1-\nu_j$. The two invariant subspaces span
$\mathbb R^m$. $\nu_j$ are real because $B^{-1}\tilde A$ is similar to the
symmetric $\tilde A^{1/2}B^{-1}\tilde A^{1/2}$ ($\tilde A\succ0$).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rank accounting]\label{thm:rank}
$Q$, $Q\Gamma$, and $I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G'$ share the same inertia (Sylvester's
law, congruence by $V_{\!o}^{1/2}$ and then by $\Gamma^{1/2}$, both nonsingular).
Hence the limiting mixture \eqref{eq:mixture} has
\begin{align}
  \#\{\text{nonzero }\lambda_j\}=\rank(Q\Gamma)
    &=(m-p)+\#\{\,j:\nu_j\neq1\,\},\label{eq:rk}\\
  \#\{\text{positive }\lambda_j\}=\np(Q\Gamma)
    &=(m-p)+\#\{\,j:\nu_j<1\,\},\label{eq:pos}\\
  \#\{\text{negative }\lambda_j\}=\nm(Q\Gamma)
    &=\phantom{(m-p)+{}}\#\{\,j:\nu_j>1\,\}.\label{eq:neg}
\end{align}
\end{theorem}

The classical chi-square picture is the special case in which all $p$ of the
$\nu_j$ equal $1$, so the $p$ model directions are exactly annihilated and only
the $m-p$ residual directions survive.

\begin{corollary}[When classical df survives]\label{cor:classical}
$\np(Q\Gamma)=\rank(Q\Gamma)=m-p$ and $\nm(Q\Gamma)=0$ if and only if
$\nu_j=1$ for all $j$, i.e.\ $B=\tilde A$. Sufficient is $B=A$ and
$\tilde A=A$, that is $K=0$ \emph{and} $W_{\!p}=V_{\!o}$ on
$\operatorname{col}(\dot\sigma)$. Both hold under correct specification ($e=0$),
and both hold for a model linear in $\theta$ with a fixed weight ($\ddot\sigma=0
\Rightarrow K=0$, $V_{\!o}=W_{\!p}\Rightarrow\tilde A=A$). When in addition
$V_{\!o}=\Gamma^{-1}$ the surviving $m-p$ weights are all $1$ and
\eqref{eq:mixture} is $\chi^2_{m-p}$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Two channels add directions]\label{cor:channels}
$\nu_j$ departs from $1$ through two independent, additive routes:
\begin{itemize}
\item the \textbf{observed-bread (curvature) channel} $B\neq A$, i.e.\ $K\neq0$:
present whenever the residual meets a curved model, $\ddot\sigma\neq0$. Factor and
path models are nonlinear in $\theta$, so $\ddot\sigma\neq0$ generically and this
channel is on for every wrong model. Absent for models linear in $\theta$.
\item the \textbf{estimated-weight (metric) channel} $\tilde A\neq A$, i.e.\
$W_{\!p}\neq V_{\!o}$: present whenever the projection metric differs from the
data metric, which is exactly when the weight is tied to the model
(ML: $W_\ast$ at the fitted covariance versus $V_0$ at the population), or to the
data and re-estimated. Absent for a fixed weight (ULS), where $V_{\!o}=W_{\!p}$
and only the curvature channel can act.
\end{itemize}
Each is $O(\lVert e\rVert)$ ($K\propto e$; $W_\ast-V_0\propto e$) and vanishes at
$H_0$. ULS exposes one channel, ML/GLS both. This is the inertia-level sibling of
the mean-level split in the companion note, where the curvature channel deforms
$\tr(U\Gamma)\to\tr(\tilde U\Gamma)$ ($A\!\to\!B$) and the weight channel is
$\beta_W$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Sign of the extra weights; indefiniteness]\label{cor:sign}
In the single-metric case write $\kappa_j=\operatorname{eig}_j(A^{-1/2}KA^{-1/2})$,
the curvature eigenvalues in the $A$-metric; then $\nu_j=(1-\kappa_j)^{-1}$, and
in the regular regime $B\succ0$ ($\kappa_j<1$),
\[
  1-\nu_j>0\iff\kappa_j<0,\qquad 1-\nu_j<0\iff\kappa_j>0 .
\]
So a \emph{negative} residual-curvature direction adds a \emph{positive}
$\chi^2_1$ weight, while a \emph{positive} one adds a \emph{negative} weight. Off
$H_0$ with positive residual-curvature directions present, $Q\Gamma$ is therefore
indefinite: the limiting law \eqref{eq:mixture} is a difference of weighted
$\chi^2_1$ variables, not the noncentral $\chi^2$ of the local-drift regime, and
not even a nonnegative mixture.
\end{corollary}

\section{Consequences for the magmaan summaries}\label{sec:magmaan}

\begin{corollary}[Reading the fields]\label{cor:magmaan}
$\texttt{spectrum\_size}=\np(Q\Gamma)=(m-p)+\#\{\nu_j<1\}\ge m-p$, with equality
exactly in the classical case of Corollary~\ref{cor:classical}. The reported
\texttt{bias\_trace} sums only the positive $\lambda_j$, whereas the principled
curvature-corrected df mean of the companion note is the \emph{signed} trace
$\tr(Q\Gamma)=\sum_j\lambda_j$. When $\nm(Q\Gamma)>0$ the two differ by
$\sum_{\lambda_j<0}|\lambda_j|$, so the positive-only \texttt{bias\_trace}
\emph{overcounts} the noise relative to $\tr(Q\Gamma)$. In the ULS check below the
gap is $3.72$ versus $2.26$.
\end{corollary}

This is a convention choice surfaced by the rank accounting, not a bug:
truncating to the positive spectrum yields a conservative (larger) trace
subtraction and a smaller RMSEA. The honest mean is the signed trace; the honest
\emph{count} of mixture terms is the positive spectrum. The two summaries answer
different questions and the note recommends carrying both, which the struct
already does.

\begin{remark}[Rank is brittle; effective rank tracks $\lVert e\rVert$]
Equations \eqref{eq:rk}--\eqref{eq:neg} are exact-arithmetic statements. For any
$e\neq0$ the generic rank is $(m-p)+\rank(\text{gap})$, but the extra
eigenvalues emerge \emph{continuously} as $O(\lVert e\rVert)$ from zero and the
integer rank jumps only at $e=0$. Near a correct model the extra weights are
numerically negligible and the classical $m-p$ description is effectively right;
far from it they are real and \eqref{eq:mixture} is genuinely higher-rank and
possibly indefinite. The reported \texttt{spectrum\_size} should be read with a
floor, as an effective rank that tracks the misfit, not as a discontinuous
integer.
\end{remark}

\section{Numerical check}\label{sec:check}

\texttt{profile\_hessian\_rank\_check.py} (numpy only) fits a one-factor model to
a two-factor population ($p^\ast=6$, $m=21$, classical $df=m-2p=9$;
$\lVert e\rVert\approx0.48$), reusing the construction of
\texttt{higher\_order\_discrepancy\_check.py}. It confirms, to machine precision:
the spectral split \eqref{eq:spectrum} ($\max|\Delta|\sim10^{-14}$); the
factorization of Lemma~\ref{lem:split}; the Sylvester inertia equality
$\In(Q\Gamma)=\In(Q)=\In(I-\tilde G B^{-1}\tilde G')$; and the counts
\eqref{eq:rk}--\eqref{eq:neg} against a direct eigendecomposition of $Q\Gamma$.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcccccc}
estimator & channels & $df$ & $\In(Q\Gamma)=(\np,\nz,\nm)$ & $\np$ & $\tr(Q\Gamma)$ & $\sum_{\lambda>0}\lambda$\\\hline
ULS (analytic $K$) & curvature      & $9$ & $(13,7,1)$ & $13$ & $2.26$ & $3.72$\\
ML  (two metric)   & curvature+weight & $9$ & $(17,1,3)$ & $17$ & $1.24$ & $10.61$\\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Both sit strictly above classical df, ULS by the one curvature channel (four
extra positive, one negative weight) and ML by both channels (eight extra
positive, three negative). A residual-shrink scan pulling the population onto the
fitted model ($\alpha:1\to0$) drives the effective rank of both down to $9$ and
the negative count to $0$ as $\lVert e\rVert\to0$, with the largest extra weight
shrinking like $O(\lVert e\rVert)$, confirming Corollaries~\ref{cor:classical}
and \ref{cor:channels} and the brittleness remark. The naive-sandwich check of
Remark~\ref{rem:envelope} reproduces $\lVert\text{sandwich}-Q\rVert=0.95\neq0$.

\section*{What remains}

The identity is structural and estimator-generic (single- and two-metric), so it
already covers ULS/GLS/WLS/DWLS and covariance ML at the level of $Q$. What it
does not yet do, and what a downstream consumer would need, is (i) an
\emph{analytic} count of $\#\{\nu_j<1\}$ from model structure rather than an
eigendecomposition, which would require signing $K$ and the metric gap a priori;
(ii) the mean-structure and FIML/ML2S two-metric $Q$, where $\tilde A$ and $B$
acquire the missing-data and mean blocks; and (iii) the categorical
$(u,\gamma)$ first-stage expansion, where the first-stage influence adds its own
directions to $\Gamma$ on top of the two channels here. Those are the open
profile-Hessian items in \texttt{docs/backlog/todo.md}; this note settles the
inertia bookkeeping they all build on.
